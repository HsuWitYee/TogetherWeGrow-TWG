About Parks

A park is a place of land set aside for people to relax, play, and enjoy nature, but it didn’t start that way long ago, parks were actually hunting grounds for kings, and over time they became open spaces where people gathered to exercise, talk, or enjoy art and gardens. As cities grew crowded in the 1800s, parks became important as peaceful green areas where anyone could escape the noise, breathe fresh air, and spend time outdoors. Today, parks look different depending on culture and climate, but they usually offer paths, trees, open fields, and sometimes theatres, zoos, sports areas, or places for boating and concerts. Overall, parks exist to give communities a shared space for recreation, nature, and connection.
